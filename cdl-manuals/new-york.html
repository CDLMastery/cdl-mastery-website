New York CDL Manual and Test Resources

Use official New York State Department of Motor Vehicles resources to prepare for your commercial learner permit, CDL knowledge tests, endorsements, and commercial road test.

Official resource guide Checked July 18, 2026

The New York State Commercial Driver’s Manual is the primary study source for New York CDL applicants. It covers safe commercial driving, cargo, air brakes, combination vehicles, endorsements, vehicle inspection, basic control skills, and on-road testing.

CDL Mastery can help you organize the material and practice individual topics, but the current New York CDL manual and official DMV instructions should remain your final authority.

Current temporary-visitor restriction New York DMV currently states that its offices cannot accept an application for a commercial learner permit or CDL from applicants who are not United States citizens or lawful permanent residents. Review the current DMV notice before applying.

Official New York CDL manual

New York DMV publishes the CDL-10 Commercial Driver’s Manual by section. The manual is available in English, Spanish, Chinese, and Russian.

New York CDL manual sections

  • Section 1 — Introduction: CDL classes, endorsements, restrictions, disqualifications, and application rules.
  • Section 2 — Driving Safely: inspections, speed, space, hazards, emergencies, railroad crossings, and difficult conditions.
  • Section 3 — Transporting Cargo Safely: weight, balance, securement, and cargo inspections.
  • Section 4 — Transporting Passengers: passenger safety, loading, unloading, and emergency procedures.
  • Section 5 — Air Brakes: components, pressure, warning systems, leakage tests, spring brakes, and safe braking.
  • Section 6 — Combination Vehicles: coupling, trailer air lines, rollover prevention, off-tracking, and inspections.
  • Section 7 — Doubles and Triples: converter dollies, coupling order, stability, and inspections.
  • Section 8 — Tank Vehicles: surge, outage, vehicle control, and tank inspection.
  • Section 9 — Hazardous Materials: placards, shipping papers, loading, parking, security, and emergencies.
  • Section 10 — School Bus: student loading, warning systems, railroad crossings, and emergency procedures.
  • Sections 11 through 13: vehicle inspection, basic vehicle control, and the on-road test.

New York CDL classes

New York issues Class A, Class B, and Class C commercial driver licenses.

  • Class A: Typically covers tractor-trailers and qualifying combinations.
  • Class B: Typically covers buses and qualifying single-unit heavy trucks.
  • Class C: Covers certain trucks, buses, stretch limousines, passenger vehicles, and hazardous-material vehicles that do not meet Class A or B weight thresholds.

Applicants must already hold a New York State Class D, Class E, or Non-CDL Class C license before applying for a New York CDL permit.

New York commercial learner permit

CDL permit tests are administered only at New York DMV offices. Applicants must pass the written tests required for the requested CDL class and endorsements.

The application process generally includes:

  1. Hold a qualifying New York State driver license.
  2. Study the current New York CDL-10 manual.
  3. Provide required identity and legal-presence documentation.
  4. Report licenses held in other jurisdictions during the prior 10 years.
  5. Complete commercial driving self-certification.
  6. Meet applicable medical-certification requirements.
  7. Pass the required knowledge and endorsement tests.
  8. Pay the applicable permit and road-test fees.
  9. Receive the New York commercial learner permit.

New York commercial learner permits are generally valid for one year.

Fourteen-day waiting period

New York does not allow applicants to schedule a commercial road test for a date within 14 days of the date the commercial learner permit was issued.

Applicable Entry-Level Driver Training must also be completed before the road test.

New York CDL knowledge tests

Required examinations depend on the requested class, endorsements, restrictions, and vehicle.

  • General or Core Knowledge.
  • Air Brakes.
  • Combination Vehicles.
  • Passenger Transport.
  • School Bus.
  • Tank Vehicles.
  • Doubles and Triples.
  • Hazardous Materials.

New York CDL class and endorsement written tests are available in English, Spanish, Chinese, and Russian.

Permit-test fees New York DMV currently lists a commercial permit application fee that covers written tests taken together during the initial application. Additional tests taken later may require another fee. Verify current amounts before applying.

Entry-Level Driver Training

Federal Entry-Level Driver Training requirements apply to many applicants seeking:

  • A first Class A CDL.
  • A first Class B CDL.
  • A Class B-to-Class A upgrade.
  • A first passenger endorsement.
  • A first school bus endorsement.
  • A first hazardous-materials endorsement.

Training must be completed through a provider listed in the FMCSA Training Provider Registry.

HazMat ELDT must be completed before taking the HazMat knowledge test. Other applicable ELDT must be completed before the CDL road test.

New York CDL medical certification

Every New York CLP and CDL holder must self-certify the type of commercial driving performed or expected.

Drivers operating in non-excepted interstate or non-excepted intrastate commerce generally must maintain a current USDOT Medical Examiner’s Certificate.

The examination must be performed by a medical examiner listed in the FMCSA National Registry of Certified Medical Examiners.

Medical examiners transmit medical-certification information electronically to New York DMV.

Keep the electronic record current An expired or missing required medical certificate can cause the driver to become medically not certified and lose commercial driving privileges. New York drivers can review their medical status through MyDMV.

New York CDL skills and road test

The commercial skills test evaluates whether the applicant can inspect, control, and safely operate a representative commercial vehicle.

  • Vehicle inspection: identifying safety-critical parts and explaining what is being checked.
  • Basic vehicle control: completing required backing and low-speed maneuvers.
  • On-road test: demonstrating safe commercial driving in traffic and roadway situations.

Applicants must take the skills test in the class and type of vehicle for which they want to be licensed.

New York permits applicants to use the official CDL Vehicle Inspection Memory Aid during the vehicle-inspection portion as long as it contains no notes or marked items.

Representative vehicle and restrictions

The commercial vehicle used during testing can affect the restrictions placed on the final CDL.

  • Testing without a full air-brake system may result in an air-brake restriction.
  • Testing in an automatic-transmission vehicle may result in a manual-transmission restriction.
  • Testing in a non-tractor-trailer Class A vehicle may result in a tractor-trailer restriction.
  • Passenger and school bus testing must be completed in an appropriate representative vehicle.

New York CDL endorsements

  • T: Doubles and triples.
  • P: Passenger vehicles.
  • N: Tank vehicles.
  • H: Hazardous materials.
  • X: Combined tank and hazardous materials.
  • S: School bus.

New York HazMat endorsement

HazMat and combined HazMat/Tank endorsements require additional federal and state qualifications.

  • Applicants must be at least 21 years old.
  • First-time applicants must complete HazMat ELDT.
  • Applicants must pass the HazMat knowledge test.
  • Applicants must provide qualifying legal-status documents.
  • Applicants must complete TSA fingerprinting and a security threat assessment.
  • Applicants must maintain required CDL and medical qualifications.

Passenger and school bus endorsements

Passenger and school bus applicants may need applicable knowledge tests, an endorsed commercial learner permit, ELDT, medical certification, skills testing, and employer or school-system qualifications.

School bus applicants should study both Section 4, Transporting Passengers, and Section 10, School Bus.

Drug and Alcohol Clearinghouse

New York DMV checks the FMCSA Drug and Alcohol Clearinghouse during applicable CLP and CDL transactions.

Drivers with a prohibited Clearinghouse status cannot operate a commercial motor vehicle. Transactions such as renewals, upgrades, duplicates, and transfers may be denied until the driver becomes compliant.

New York can downgrade a CDL or cancel a CLP when a prohibited driver does not complete the federal return-to-duty process within the required period.

How to study the New York CDL manual

  1. Choose the correct CDL class. Determine whether you need Class A, B, or C.
  2. Identify every endorsement. List HazMat, Tank, Passenger, School Bus, or Doubles and Triples.
  3. Study Sections 1 through 3 first. Build your foundation in licensing, safe driving, inspections, and cargo.
  4. Add Air Brakes when applicable. Learn system components, pressure rules, warning systems, leakage limits, and inspection steps.
  5. Add Combination Vehicles for Class A. Focus on coupling, trailer air systems, rollover prevention, off-tracking, and inspections.
  6. Study endorsements separately. Keep HazMat, Tank, Passenger, School Bus, and Doubles and Triples in focused sessions.
  7. Use the official knowledge-review questions. New York DMV provides a summary of the manual’s Test Your Knowledge questions.
  8. Prepare separately for the road test. Practice the inspection aloud, basic control maneuvers, and on-road habits in a representative commercial vehicle.

New York CDL preparation checklist

  • Read the current New York CDL-10 Manual.
  • Confirm your CDL class and endorsements.
  • Hold a qualifying New York driver license.
  • Prepare identity and legal-presence documents.
  • Report required prior licensing history.
  • Complete commercial driving self-certification.
  • Confirm whether medical certification is required.
  • Check whether ELDT applies.
  • Pass all applicable knowledge tests.
  • Obtain and hold the CLP for the required period.
  • Practice vehicle inspection language aloud.
  • Practice basic control maneuvers.
  • Pay the road-test fee.
  • Schedule the New York commercial road test.
