New Jersey CDL Manual and Test Resources

Use official New Jersey Motor Vehicle Commission resources to prepare for your commercial learner’s permit, CDL knowledge examinations, endorsements, and commercial skills test.

Official resource guide Checked July 18, 2026

The New Jersey Commercial Driver License Manual is the primary study source for New Jersey CDL applicants. It covers commercial licensing, safe driving, cargo, air brakes, combination vehicles, endorsements, vehicle inspection, basic control skills, and road testing.

CDL Mastery can help you organize the material and practice individual topics, but the current New Jersey CDL manual and official NJMVC instructions should remain your final authority.

Current medical-certification process New Jersey now receives Medical Examiner’s Certificate information electronically from the FMCSA National Registry. NJMVC no longer accepts routine paper medical certificates for CDL and CLP transactions.

Official New Jersey CDL manual

NJMVC publishes the Commercial Driver Manual through its official driver-manuals and CDL-manual pages. The current commercial manual is identified by NJMVC as revised March 2025.

Major New Jersey CDL manual topics

The sections you need depend on the CDL class, vehicle equipment, endorsements, and type of commercial operation.

  • Introduction and licensing: CDL classes, endorsements, restrictions, disqualifications, and application rules.
  • Driving Safely: inspections, speed, space management, hazards, railroad crossings, emergencies, and difficult conditions.
  • Transporting Cargo Safely: weight, balance, securement, cargo inspections, and load-related risks.
  • Passenger Vehicles: loading, unloading, prohibited practices, emergency equipment, and passenger safety.
  • Air Brakes: system components, pressure, warning devices, spring brakes, leakage testing, and safe braking.
  • Combination Vehicles: coupling systems, fifth wheels, trailer air lines, rollover prevention, off-tracking, and inspections.
  • Doubles and Triples: converter dollies, coupling order, handling, stability, and inspections.
  • Tank Vehicles: liquid surge, outage, tank inspection, and safe vehicle control.
  • Hazardous Materials: placards, shipping papers, loading, parking, security, and emergency procedures.
  • School Buses: student loading, warning devices, railroad crossings, inspections, and emergency procedures.
  • Skills testing: vehicle inspection, basic control skills, and the road test.

New Jersey CDL classes

New Jersey issues Class A, Class B, and Class C commercial driver licenses.

  • Class A: Covers qualifying combinations with a gross combination weight rating or weight of at least 26,001 pounds when the towed unit exceeds 10,000 pounds.
  • Class B: Covers qualifying single vehicles with a gross vehicle weight rating, registered weight, or actual gross weight of at least 26,001 pounds.
  • Class C: Covers certain vehicles transporting placardable hazardous materials, vehicles designed for 16 or more people including the driver, certain school-related transportation, and some for-hire passenger vehicles.

New Jersey CDL eligibility

Applicants must generally meet the following basic qualifications:

  • Be at least 18 years old.
  • Hold a basic New Jersey Class D driver license.
  • Meet applicable citizenship or lawful-status requirements.
  • Meet New Jersey identity and residency requirements.
  • Have at least 20/40 vision in each eye, with or without lenses.
  • Be able to recognize red, green, and amber.
  • Meet applicable medical requirements.

Applicants should review the current NJMVC eligibility page before applying because federal and state lawful-status rules may change.

New Jersey age restrictions

Applicants may qualify for a New Jersey CDL beginning at age 18. Drivers younger than 21 are limited to intrastate commercial operation within New Jersey.

Under age 21 New Jersey CDL applicants younger than 21 must select intrastate commerce category 3 or 4. They may not receive a HazMat endorsement or passenger endorsement.

New Jersey commercial learner’s permit

New Jersey issues the commercial learner’s permit as a secure digital credential rather than a paper permit.

The CLP is generally valid for 180 days and must be accompanied by the applicant’s New Jersey driver license when operating a commercial vehicle.

The application process generally includes:

  1. Hold a valid New Jersey Class D driver license.
  2. Study the current New Jersey CDL manual.
  3. Purchase and complete the required permit or test receipt process.
  4. Provide required identity and eligibility documentation.
  5. Complete the CDL Holder Self-Certification form.
  6. Meet applicable medical-certification requirements.
  7. Pass the required CDL knowledge examinations.
  8. Have the test receipt validated at a licensing center.
  9. Receive the commercial learner’s permit.

Fourteen-day practice requirement

Unless a valid waiver applies, New Jersey CLP holders must practice for at least 14 days before taking the CDL skills test.

Practice must be completed with a properly licensed CDL holder who is qualified to operate the vehicle and supervise the CLP holder.

The applicant must carry the current validated CLP while practicing.

New Jersey CDL knowledge tests

The required knowledge examinations depend on the requested CDL class, endorsements, restrictions, and vehicle.

Possible tests include:

  • General Knowledge.
  • Air Brakes.
  • Combination Vehicles.
  • Passenger Transport.
  • School Bus.
  • Tank Vehicles.
  • Doubles and Triples.
  • Hazardous Materials.

New Jersey states that completed knowledge-test scores are generally valid for 360 days.

Test receipt fee NJMVC currently lists a non-refundable commercial permit or test receipt fee. Review the current fee and qualifications before paying.

Entry-Level Driver Training

Federal Entry-Level Driver Training requirements apply to many first-time CDL applicants, upgrades, and first-time endorsement applicants.

When applicable, training must be completed through a provider listed in the FMCSA Training Provider Registry.

  • HazMat endorsement: Required ELDT must be completed before the HazMat knowledge test.
  • First-time CDL classes and upgrades: Required ELDT must be completed before the applicable skills test.
  • Passenger and school bus endorsements: Required ELDT must be completed before the applicable skills test.

New Jersey CDL medical certification

CDL holders and CLP applicants must complete the CDL Holder Self-Certification form and select the commerce category that matches their operation.

Drivers certifying as non-excepted interstate or non-excepted intrastate generally must have a valid Medical Examiner’s Certificate recorded in the FMCSA National Registry.

New Jersey requires passenger and passenger/school bus endorsement holders to maintain an electronic medical certificate regardless of their selected commerce category.

Medical examiners must submit examination results electronically to the National Registry. The medical information is then transferred to NJMVC.

Do not submit a routine paper certificate NJMVC announced in March 2026 that it no longer accepts paper Medical Examiner’s Certificates for this process. Drivers should contact the medical examiner if the electronic record is missing or incorrect.

NJMVC recommends allowing approximately 24 hours before checking the status of a newly transmitted medical certificate.

Failure to maintain required self-certification or medical certification can cause the CDL to be decertified or downgraded.

New Jersey CDL skills test

The New Jersey CDL skills test evaluates whether the applicant can inspect, control, and safely operate a representative commercial vehicle.

The test generally includes:

  • Vehicle inspection: identifying safety-critical components and explaining the condition being checked.
  • Basic control skills: completing required low-speed control and backing maneuvers.
  • Road test: demonstrating safe commercial driving in traffic and roadway situations.

Applicants must schedule an appointment for the skills test and use an appropriate representative vehicle.

ELDT completion and the 14-day CLP practice period must be satisfied before the test when applicable.

Representative vehicle and restrictions

The vehicle used for the CDL skills test affects the license class, endorsements, and restrictions placed on the CDL.

  • Testing without a full air-brake system may result in an air-brake restriction.
  • Testing in an automatic-transmission vehicle may result in a manual-transmission restriction.
  • Testing in a vehicle that does not represent the desired Class A tractor-trailer configuration may result in a restriction.
  • Passenger and school bus testing must use an appropriate representative passenger vehicle.

New Jersey CDL endorsements

Common New Jersey CDL endorsements include:

  • T: Double and triple trailers.
  • P: Passenger vehicles.
  • N: Tank vehicles.
  • H: Hazardous materials.
  • X: Combined tank and hazardous materials.
  • S: School bus operation.

New Jersey HazMat endorsement

A hazardous materials endorsement requires additional federal and state steps.

The process may include:

  • First-time HazMat Entry-Level Driver Training.
  • Passing the New Jersey HazMat knowledge examination.
  • Providing required identity and lawful-status documents.
  • Completing TSA fingerprinting and a security threat assessment.
  • Maintaining required medical and CDL qualifications.

Drivers younger than 21 are not eligible for a New Jersey HazMat endorsement.

Passenger and school bus requirements

Passenger and school bus applicants may need applicable knowledge tests, endorsed CLP privileges, ELDT, skills testing, medical certification, fingerprinting, background checks, and employer or education-agency qualifications.

New Jersey provides a fingerprinting application for commercial bus drivers through its official forms section.

Drivers younger than 21 are not eligible for a passenger endorsement in New Jersey.

How to study the New Jersey CDL manual

  1. Choose the correct CDL class. Determine whether you need Class A, B, or C.
  2. Identify every endorsement. List HazMat, Tank, Passenger, School Bus, or Doubles and Triples.
  3. Study General Knowledge first. Build your foundation in inspections, speed, space, hazards, and emergencies.
  4. Add Air Brakes when applicable. Learn system components, warning devices, pressure rules, leakage limits, and inspection steps.
  5. Add Combination Vehicles for Class A. Focus on coupling, trailer air systems, rollover prevention, off-tracking, and inspections.
  6. Study endorsements separately. Keep HazMat, Tank, Passenger, School Bus, and Doubles and Triples in focused sessions.
  7. Practice by topic. Use missed questions to identify the exact manual section that needs review.
  8. Prepare separately for skills testing. Practice vehicle-inspection language, basic control maneuvers, and road-test habits in a representative vehicle.

New Jersey CDL preparation checklist

  • Read the current New Jersey CDL Manual.
  • Confirm your CDL class and endorsements.
  • Hold a valid New Jersey Class D license.
  • Prepare identity and eligibility documents.
  • Complete CDL self-certification.
  • Confirm whether medical certification is required.
  • Check whether ELDT applies.
  • Pass the applicable knowledge examinations.
  • Validate the required test receipt.
  • Obtain the digital CLP.
  • Practice with a qualified CDL holder for at least 14 days.
  • Practice the vehicle inspection aloud.
  • Practice basic control maneuvers.
  • Schedule the New Jersey CDL skills test.
