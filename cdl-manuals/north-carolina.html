North Carolina CDL Manual and Test Resources

Use official North Carolina Division of Motor Vehicles resources to prepare for your commercial learner permit, CDL knowledge tests, endorsements, and commercial driving skills test.

Official resource guide Checked July 18, 2026

The North Carolina Commercial Driver License Manual is the primary study source for North Carolina CDL applicants. It explains commercial licensing, safe driving, cargo, air brakes, combination vehicles, endorsements, vehicle inspection, basic control skills, and road-test preparation.

CDL Mastery can help you organize and practice the material, but the current NCDMV manual and official state instructions should remain your final authority.

Commercial road-test appointments North Carolina requires applicants seeking a commercial driver license road test to call NCDMV and schedule an appointment.

Major North Carolina CDL manual topics

The sections you need depend on your requested CDL class, vehicle, endorsements, restrictions, and type of commercial operation.

  • Introduction and licensing: CDL classes, endorsements, restrictions, disqualifications, and application rules.
  • Driving Safely: inspections, vehicle control, speed, following distance, hazards, emergencies, railroad crossings, and difficult conditions.
  • Transporting Cargo Safely: weight, balance, securement, inspections, and load-related risks.
  • Passenger Vehicles: loading, unloading, emergency equipment, prohibited practices, and passenger safety.
  • Air Brakes: air-system components, pressure, warning devices, spring brakes, leakage checks, and safe braking.
  • Combination Vehicles: coupling systems, fifth wheels, trailer air lines, rollover prevention, off-tracking, and inspections.
  • Doubles and Triples: converter dollies, coupling order, stability, handling, and inspections.
  • Tank Vehicles: liquid surge, outage, tank inspection, and vehicle control.
  • Hazardous Materials: placards, shipping papers, loading, parking, security, and emergency procedures.
  • School Buses: student loading, warning systems, railroad crossings, inspections, and emergencies.
  • Skills testing: vehicle inspection, basic control skills, and on-road driving.

North Carolina CDL classes

North Carolina issues Class A, Class B, and Class C commercial driver licenses.

  • Class A: Generally covers qualifying combinations with a gross combination weight rating of at least 26,001 pounds when the towed vehicle exceeds 10,000 pounds.
  • Class B: Generally covers qualifying single vehicles with a gross vehicle weight rating of at least 26,001 pounds and certain vehicles towing 10,000 pounds or less.
  • Class C: Covers certain vehicles outside Class A or B that transport placardable hazardous materials or qualifying numbers of passengers.

North Carolina age requirements

Applicants must be at least 18 years old to obtain a North Carolina commercial learner permit for qualifying intrastate operation.

Age 21 requirement Drivers must generally be at least 21 years old to operate in interstate commerce or obtain a hazardous materials endorsement.

North Carolina commercial learner permit

Federal and state law require applicants to pass the applicable knowledge examinations before receiving a commercial learner permit.

A North Carolina CLP is valid for 180 days and may be renewed once for another 180-day period.

The application process generally includes:

  1. Be at least 18 years old.
  2. Hold a valid North Carolina Class A, B, or C driver license.
  3. Provide proof of identity and age.
  4. Provide proof of Social Security number.
  5. Provide the required North Carolina residency documents.
  6. Provide proof of liability insurance when required.
  7. Complete commercial driving self-certification.
  8. Meet applicable medical requirements.
  9. Pass the vision and traffic-sign recognition tests.
  10. Pass all required CDL knowledge examinations.

NCDMV sends the secure permit by mail. Applicants receive a temporary driving certificate to use until the permit arrives.

Fourteen-day waiting period

North Carolina requires applicants to hold the commercial learner permit for at least 14 days before taking the CDL skills test.

While practicing, the permit holder must be accompanied in the front seat by a properly licensed CDL holder with the appropriate class and endorsements.

The exact knowledge tests depend on the requested CDL class, endorsements, restrictions, and vehicle equipment.

  • General Knowledge.

Entry-Level Driver Training

Federal Entry-Level Driver Training requirements apply to many applicants seeking:

  • A first Class A CDL.
  • A first Class B CDL.
  • A Class B-to-Class A upgrade.
  • A first passenger endorsement.
  • A first school bus endorsement.
  • A first hazardous materials endorsement.

Required training must be completed through a provider listed in the FMCSA Training Provider Registry.

HazMat ELDT must be completed before the HazMat knowledge test. Applicable Class A, Class B, passenger, and school bus training must be completed before the CDL skills test.

North Carolina CDL medical certification

North Carolina commercial drivers must identify the type of commerce in which they operate or expect to operate.

The four self-certification categories are:

  • Interstate non-excepted.
  • Interstate excepted.
  • Intrastate non-excepted.
  • Intrastate excepted.

Interstate non-excepted and intrastate non-excepted drivers generally must maintain a certified medical status.

Maintain your medical status A required medical certification that expires or is missing can affect the driver’s North Carolina commercial driving privileges. Confirm current submission instructions directly with NCDMV.

North Carolina CDL skills test

The North Carolina CDL skills test evaluates whether the applicant can inspect, control, and safely operate a representative commercial vehicle.

The test generally includes:

  • Vehicle inspection: identifying safety-critical parts and explaining what is being checked.
  • Basic control skills: completing required low-speed vehicle-control and backing maneuvers.
  • Road test: demonstrating safe commercial driving in traffic and roadway situations.

Applicants must bring an appropriate representative vehicle for the desired CDL class and endorsements.

To schedule a North Carolina commercial road test, applicants must call NCDMV at 919-715-7000.

Representative vehicle and restrictions

The vehicle used during the skills test affects the class, endorsements, and restrictions placed on the final CDL.

  • Testing without a full air-brake system may result in an air-brake restriction.
  • Testing in an automatic-transmission vehicle may result in a manual-transmission restriction.
  • Testing in a vehicle that does not represent the desired Class A tractor-trailer configuration may result in a restriction.
  • Passenger and school bus applicants must use an appropriate representative passenger vehicle.

North Carolina CDL endorsements

Common endorsements include:

  • T: Doubles and triples.
  • P: Passenger vehicles.
  • N: Tank vehicles.
  • H: Hazardous materials.
  • X: Combined tank and hazardous materials.
  • S: School bus operation.

North Carolina HazMat endorsement

A hazardous materials endorsement requires additional state and federal steps.

  • Be at least 21 years old.
  • Complete first-time HazMat ELDT when applicable.
  • Pass the North Carolina HazMat knowledge test.
  • Provide required identity and lawful-status documentation.
  • Complete TSA fingerprinting and a security threat assessment.
  • Maintain applicable CDL and medical qualifications.

Applicants may be able to use an eligible Transportation Worker Identification Credential security assessment as a comparable security threat assessment when allowed by current federal and state rules.

Passenger and school bus requirements

Passenger and school bus applicants may need applicable knowledge tests, endorsed CLP privileges, ELDT, medical certification, skills testing, background checks, and employer or school-system qualifications.

School bus applicants should study both the Passenger Vehicles and School Buses sections of the North Carolina commercial manual.

Military CDL skills-test waiver

Qualifying active-duty service members and veterans with recent experience operating military vehicles equivalent to commercial vehicles may apply for a waiver of the CDL skills test.

Applicants must still satisfy applicable eligibility, medical, documentation, and knowledge-test requirements.

How to study the North Carolina CDL manual

  1. Choose your CDL class. Determine whether you need Class A, B, or C.
  2. Identify every endorsement. List HazMat, Tank, Passenger, School Bus, or Doubles and Triples.
  3. Study General Knowledge first. Build your foundation in inspections, speed, space, hazards, and emergencies.
  4. Add Air Brakes when applicable. Learn the components, warning devices, pressure rules, leakage limits, and inspection procedure.
  5. Add Combination Vehicles for Class A. Focus on coupling, trailer air systems, rollover prevention, off-tracking, and inspections.
  6. Study endorsements separately. Keep HazMat, Tank, Passenger, School Bus, and Doubles and Triples in focused sessions.
  7. Practice by topic. Use missed questions to identify the exact manual section that needs review.
  8. Prepare separately for the skills test. Practice inspection language, control maneuvers, and road-test habits in a representative vehicle.

North Carolina CDL preparation checklist

  • Read the current North Carolina CDL Manual.
  • Confirm your CDL class and endorsements.
  • Prepare identity and residency documents.
  • Provide proof of Social Security number.
  • Provide proof of insurance when required.
  • Complete commercial driving self-certification.
  • Confirm whether medical certification is required.
  • Check whether ELDT applies.
  • Pass all applicable knowledge examinations.
  • Obtain and hold the CLP for at least 14 days.
  • Practice the vehicle inspection aloud.
  • Practice basic vehicle-control maneuvers.
  • Call NCDMV to schedule the commercial skills test.
