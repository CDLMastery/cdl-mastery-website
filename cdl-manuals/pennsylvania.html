Pennsylvania CDL Manual and Test Resources

Use official PennDOT materials to prepare for your commercial learner’s permit, knowledge tests, endorsements, and CDL skills test.

Official Pennsylvania CDL manual

PennDOT publishes the Pennsylvania Commercial Driver’s Manual as its official guide for commercial driver applicants. The manual contains the information needed for the general knowledge test and applicable endorsement or restriction-removal tests.

Study only the sections required for the vehicle and driving privileges you plan to obtain, while remembering that all CDL applicants need the core general knowledge material.

Major manual sections to study

Your required sections depend on the class of license, vehicle equipment, endorsements, and restrictions involved.

  • General Knowledge: inspections, vehicle control, shifting, speed management, space management, hazards, emergencies, and safe driving.
  • Air Brakes: air-system components, warning devices, spring brakes, inspections, leakage testing, and safe braking.
  • Combination Vehicles: coupling, fifth wheels, trailer air lines, off-tracking, rollover risk, trailer braking, and uncoupling.
  • Transporting Cargo: weight distribution, securement, inspections, and load-related handling risks.
  • Tank Vehicles: surge, outage, vehicle handling, and liquid-load safety.
  • Doubles and Triples: converter dollies, coupling order, inspections, handling, and emergency situations.
  • Passenger and School Bus: passenger safety, loading, unloading, emergency procedures, and vehicle-specific inspections.
  • Hazardous Materials: communication rules, placards, shipping papers, loading, parking, security, and emergency response.

Applying for a Pennsylvania commercial learner’s permit

Pennsylvania applicants generally begin by submitting the appropriate commercial learner’s permit application, self-certification information, required documentation, medical information when applicable, and fees.

PennDOT then issues a Knowledge Test Authorization for each requested CDL class, endorsement, or restriction removal. The applicant uses those authorizations to complete the required knowledge testing.

Common application documents

Required documents depend on the applicant’s situation and type of commercial operation. PennDOT’s current instructions should be checked before visiting a Driver License Center.

  • Commercial Learner’s Permit Application.
  • CDL self-certification form.
  • Current Pennsylvania driver’s license or accepted identification.
  • Proof of identity and Pennsylvania residency when required.
  • Social Security documentation when required.
  • Medical Examiner’s Certificate for applicable operations.
  • Required fees in an accepted payment form.
  • Additional documents for endorsements such as HazMat.

Pennsylvania CDL knowledge tests

Every Pennsylvania CDL applicant must pass the knowledge tests required for the desired license class, endorsements, and restriction removals. The exact combination of tests varies by applicant.

Possible knowledge tests include:

  • General Knowledge.
  • Air Brakes.
  • Combination Vehicles.
  • Passenger.
  • School Bus.
  • Tank Vehicles.
  • Doubles and Triples.
  • Hazardous Materials.
Check testing hours before traveling PennDOT location services indicate that CDL knowledge testing must be started before the location’s stated cutoff time. Confirm current hours, services, and testing availability for the specific center you plan to visit.

The Pennsylvania CDL skills test

After meeting permit, training, and eligibility requirements, applicants must complete the applicable CDL skills test in a representative vehicle.

The test generally evaluates three broad areas:

  • Vehicle inspection: identifying components, checking their condition, and explaining safety concerns.
  • Basic control skills: demonstrating required low-speed vehicle-control maneuvers.
  • On-road driving: safely operating the commercial vehicle in real traffic conditions.

Pennsylvania uses the modernized CDL skills-testing model at PennDOT testing locations and authorized third-party testers.

Entry-Level Driver Training

Federal Entry-Level Driver Training requirements may apply before an eligible applicant can take certain CDL skills or endorsement tests. This commonly includes first-time Class A or Class B applicants, upgrades from Class B to Class A, and certain first-time endorsements.

Training subject to ELDT must be completed through a provider listed on the federal Training Provider Registry.

Pennsylvania HazMat applicants

A hazardous materials endorsement involves additional federal and state requirements beyond the standard CDL knowledge tests. These may include identity verification, a Transportation Security Administration security threat assessment, fingerprints, application requirements, and recurring testing or renewal procedures.

Applicants should follow PennDOT’s current HazMat instructions rather than relying on general or outdated third-party summaries.

Age and operating limits

Commercial driving privileges may depend on the driver’s age and type of operation. Pennsylvania notes that interstate commercial motor vehicle operation requires the driver to be at least 21 years old.

Applicants should verify age, interstate, intrastate, passenger, school bus, and hazardous-material limitations before selecting a CDL path.

How to study the Pennsylvania manual

  1. Identify your CDL goal. Determine whether you need Class A, B, or C and which endorsements apply.
  2. List every required manual section. Do not spend equal time on sections unrelated to your intended vehicle or endorsements.
  3. Study General Knowledge first. Build a foundation in inspections, hazard recognition, speed, space, and emergencies.
  4. Add vehicle-system sections. Study Air Brakes and Combination Vehicles when applicable.
  5. Add endorsement sections. Focus separately on Tanker, Doubles and Triples, Passenger, School Bus, or HazMat.
  6. Practice by topic. Use questions to reveal weak areas instead of memorizing answers.
  7. Review every mistake in the manual. Find the rule, understand why it exists, and explain it in your own words.
  8. Finish with mixed practice. Practice switching between subjects before taking the real test.
