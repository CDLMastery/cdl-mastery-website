Ohio CDL Manual and Test Resources

Use official Ohio Bureau of Motor Vehicles resources to prepare for your commercial learner permit, CDL knowledge examinations, endorsements, and commercial skills test.

Official resource guide Checked July 18, 2026

The Ohio Commercial Driver License Manual is the primary study source for Ohio CDL applicants. It covers commercial licensing, safe driving, cargo, air brakes, combination vehicles, endorsements, vehicle inspection, basic control skills, and road testing.

CDL Mastery can help you organize and practice the material, but the current Ohio CDL manual and official Ohio BMV instructions should remain your final authority.

Current eligibility documentation First-time Ohio CDL applicants must provide qualifying proof of United States citizenship or lawful permanent residency. Review the current Ohio BMV document list before visiting a testing or licensing location.

Official Ohio CDL manual

Ohio BMV publishes the Commercial Driver License Manual as form HSY 7605. A Spanish edition is also available as HSY 7606.

Major Ohio CDL manual topics

The sections you must study depend on the requested CDL class, vehicle equipment, endorsements, restrictions, and commercial operation.

  • Introduction and licensing: CDL classes, endorsements, restrictions, disqualifications, and application requirements.
  • Driving Safely: inspections, speed, space management, hazards, emergencies, railroad crossings, and difficult driving conditions.
  • Transporting Cargo Safely: cargo weight, balance, securement, inspections, and load-related risks.
  • Passenger Vehicles: loading, unloading, emergency equipment, prohibited practices, and passenger protection.
  • Air Brakes: air-system components, pressure, warning devices, spring brakes, leakage tests, and safe braking.
  • Combination Vehicles: coupling, fifth wheels, trailer air lines, rollover prevention, off-tracking, and inspections.
  • Doubles and Triples: converter dollies, coupling order, handling, stability, and inspections.
  • Tank Vehicles: liquid surge, outage, tank inspection, and vehicle control.
  • Hazardous Materials: placards, shipping papers, loading, parking, security, and emergencies.
  • School Buses: student loading, warning systems, railroad crossings, inspections, and emergency procedures.
  • Skills testing: pre-trip inspection, basic maneuverability, and the road test.

Ohio CDL classes

Ohio issues Class A, Class B, and Class C commercial driver licenses.

  • Class A: Covers qualifying combinations with a gross combination weight rating of at least 26,001 pounds when the towed vehicle exceeds 10,000 pounds.
  • Class B: Covers qualifying single vehicles with a gross vehicle weight rating of at least 26,001 pounds and certain vehicles towing no more than 10,000 pounds.
  • Class C: Covers certain vehicles outside Class A or B that transport placardable hazardous materials or qualifying numbers of passengers.

Ohio CDL eligibility

First-time Ohio CDL applicants must generally:

  • Be at least 18 years old.
  • Hold a valid Ohio driver license.
  • Have a valid United States Social Security number.
  • Provide qualifying proof of United States citizenship or lawful permanent residency.
  • Pass the applicable vision or physical qualifications.
  • Pass the required CDL knowledge tests.
  • Complete commercial driving self-certification.
  • Meet applicable medical-certification requirements.

Ohio age requirements

Applicants may qualify for an Ohio commercial learner permit or CDL beginning at age 18.

Under age 21 Ohio CDL holders younger than 21 are restricted to intrastate commercial driving and may operate only within Ohio.

Ohio commercial learner permit

First-time applicants must pass the required knowledge examinations and purchase an Ohio commercial learner permit before practicing in a commercial motor vehicle.

The process generally includes:

  1. Hold a valid Ohio driver license.
  2. Study the current Ohio CDL Manual.
  3. Gather identity and legal-status documents.
  4. Provide a valid Social Security number.
  5. Complete CDL self-certification.
  6. Meet applicable medical qualifications.
  7. Pass the required knowledge tests at a driver examination station.
  8. Purchase the commercial learner permit.
  9. Complete applicable Entry-Level Driver Training.
  10. Practice with a properly licensed CDL holder.

The applicant must hold a permit containing the appropriate class and endorsements for the commercial vehicle used during practice and testing.

Fourteen-day permit period

Federal CDL rules generally require applicants to hold the commercial learner permit for at least 14 days before completing the applicable CDL skills test.

Applicants must also complete any required Entry-Level Driver Training before scheduling or attempting the Ohio skills test.

Ohio CDL knowledge tests

Knowledge examinations are administered in person at Ohio driver examination locations.

Possible examinations include:

  • General Knowledge.
  • Air Brakes.
  • Combination Vehicles.
  • Passenger Transport.
  • School Bus.
  • Tank Vehicles.
  • Doubles and Triples.
  • Hazardous Materials.

The exact tests depend on the requested license class, endorsements, vehicle equipment, and restrictions.

Use the current manual Practice questions can identify weak areas, but the official Ohio CDL Manual contains the complete rules, inspection steps, and safety concepts used during testing.

Entry-Level Driver Training

Federal Entry-Level Driver Training requirements apply to many applicants seeking:

  • A first Class A CDL.
  • A first Class B CDL.
  • A Class B-to-Class A upgrade.
  • A first passenger endorsement.
  • A first school bus endorsement.
  • A first hazardous materials endorsement.

Training must be completed through a provider listed in the FMCSA Training Provider Registry.

Ohio requires electronic proof of applicable ELDT completion before the applicant schedules or attempts the skills test.

First-time HazMat applicants must complete the applicable HazMat ELDT before taking the HazMat knowledge examination.

Ohio CDL medical certification

Ohio CLP applicants and CDL holders must self-certify the type of commercial driving they perform or expect to perform.

The four self-certification categories are:

  • Non-excepted interstate.
  • Excepted interstate.
  • Non-excepted intrastate.
  • Excepted intrastate.

Drivers in a non-excepted category generally must maintain a valid medical certification when required by federal or Ohio rules.

Effective June 23, 2025, certified medical examiners began transmitting commercial-driver examination results electronically through the FMCSA National Registry to Ohio BMV.

Paper medical cards generally are not required Ohio CDL and CLP holders generally no longer need to fax, mail, or personally deliver routine DOT medical cards when the examiner has transmitted the result electronically. Confirm that your Ohio record updates correctly after the examination.

Failure to maintain required medical certification or self-certification can prevent issuance, renewal, transfer, or upgrade of commercial privileges and may lead to a CDL downgrade.

Ohio CDL skills test

Ohio’s CDL skills test consists of three parts.

  • Pre-trip inspection: Identifying safety-critical components and explaining the condition being checked. Ohio currently lists a 30-minute time limit.
  • Basic maneuverability: Completing required low-speed control and backing exercises. Ohio currently lists a 40-minute time limit.
  • Road test: Demonstrating safe commercial driving in traffic and roadway situations.

Applicants must provide a vehicle appropriate for the requested CDL class and endorsements.

Passenger applicants must provide an appropriate bus designed to transport at least 16 people, including the driver, when required for the endorsement test.

Retesting after a failure Ohio requires an applicant who fails any portion of the CDL skills test to wait two days before retesting.

Ohio CDL skills-test fees

At an Ohio state-owned testing facility, applicants must first purchase the required CDL Test Receipt from a deputy registrar license agency.

Ohio currently lists these state-facility testing fees:

  • Pre-trip inspection: $10.
  • Basic maneuverability test: $10.
  • Road test: $30.

Third-party testing facilities establish their own fees, which may be different from state-owned facility fees.

Ohio CDL skills-test locations

CDL skills testing is available by appointment through state-owned and approved third-party testing locations.

Third-party providers may offer testing vehicles for rent, but availability and fees vary. Confirm the vehicle, appointment, payment, and documentation requirements directly with the location.

Representative vehicle and restrictions

The vehicle used during the Ohio CDL skills test can affect the restrictions placed on the final license.

  • Testing without a full air-brake system may result in an air-brake restriction.
  • Testing in an automatic-transmission vehicle may result in a manual-transmission restriction.
  • Testing in a Class A vehicle that is not a tractor-trailer may result in a tractor-trailer restriction.
  • Passenger and school bus applicants must use an appropriate representative vehicle.

Applicants should test in equipment matching the commercial vehicle they expect to operate whenever possible.

Ohio CDL endorsements

Common Ohio CDL endorsements include:

  • T: Double and triple trailers.
  • P: Passenger vehicles.
  • N: Tank vehicles.
  • H: Hazardous materials.
  • X: Combined tank and hazardous materials.
  • S: School bus operation.

Ohio HazMat endorsement

A hazardous materials endorsement requires additional state and federal qualifications.

  • Be at least 21 years old.
  • Complete first-time HazMat ELDT when applicable.
  • Pass the Ohio HazMat knowledge test.
  • Provide required identity and lawful-status documentation.
  • Complete TSA fingerprinting and a security threat assessment.
  • Maintain required CDL and medical qualifications.

Applicants transferring an out-of-state CDL with a HazMat endorsement should review the remaining validity of their TSA security threat assessment before beginning the Ohio transfer process.

Passenger and school bus requirements

Passenger and school bus applicants may need applicable knowledge tests, endorsed permit privileges, ELDT, medical certification, skills testing, background checks, and employer or school-system qualifications.

School bus applicants should study both the Passenger Vehicles and School Buses sections of the Ohio CDL manual.

Drug and Alcohol Clearinghouse

Ohio checks the FMCSA Drug and Alcohol Clearinghouse during applicable CLP and CDL transactions.

Ohio cannot issue, renew, upgrade, or transfer commercial privileges when the applicant is prohibited from operating a commercial motor vehicle because of a Clearinghouse status.

Out-of-state CDL applicants

Drivers with an unexpired out-of-state CDL may apply to transfer their commercial privileges to Ohio by presenting the required identity, citizenship or residency, Social Security, Ohio address, medical, and self-certification documentation.

Out-of-state CLP holders may qualify to complete Ohio skills testing when they meet current Ohio and federal conditions, including applicable ELDT verification.

Special validation requirements may apply to non-domiciled commercial learner permits under federal rules updated in 2026.

How to study the Ohio CDL manual

  1. Choose the correct CDL class. Determine whether you need Class A, B, or C.
  2. Identify every endorsement. List HazMat, Tank, Passenger, School Bus, or Doubles and Triples.
  3. Study General Knowledge first. Build your foundation in inspections, speed, space, hazards, and emergencies.
  4. Add Air Brakes when applicable. Learn system components, pressure rules, warning devices, leakage limits, and inspection steps.
  5. Add Combination Vehicles for Class A. Focus on coupling, trailer air systems, rollover prevention, off-tracking, and inspections.
  6. Study endorsements separately. Keep HazMat, Tank, Passenger, School Bus, and Doubles and Triples in focused sessions.
  7. Practice by topic. Use missed questions to identify the exact manual section that needs review.
  8. Prepare separately for skills testing. Practice the pre-trip inspection aloud, basic maneuverability, and safe road-test habits in a representative vehicle.

Ohio CDL preparation checklist

  • Read the current Ohio CDL Manual.
  • Confirm your CDL class and endorsements.
  • Hold a valid Ohio driver license.
  • Prepare citizenship or lawful permanent residency documents.
  • Provide a valid Social Security number.
  • Complete CDL self-certification.
  • Confirm whether medical certification is required.
  • Check whether ELDT applies.
  • Pass all applicable knowledge tests.
  • Purchase the commercial learner permit.
  • Hold and practice with the CLP for the required period.
  • Practice the pre-trip inspection aloud.
  • Practice basic maneuverability exercises.
  • Schedule an Ohio CDL skills test.
  • Purchase the CDL before the CLP expires after passing.
