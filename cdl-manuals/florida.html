Florida CDL Manual and Test Resources

Use official Florida Highway Safety and Motor Vehicles resources to prepare for your commercial learner’s permit, CDL knowledge tests, endorsements, and commercial skills test.

Official resource guide Checked July 17, 2026

The Florida CDL Handbook is the primary study source for Florida commercial driver license applicants. It covers safe commercial driving, cargo, air brakes, combination vehicles, endorsements, vehicle inspection, basic control skills, and road-test preparation.

CDL Mastery can help you organize the material and practice individual topics, but the current Florida handbook and official FLHSMV instructions should remain your final authority.

Florida residency requirement Applicants seeking an original Florida commercial learner’s permit or commercial driver license must meet Florida residency and residential address-document requirements. Review the current FLHSMV document rules before visiting an office.

Major Florida CDL handbook topics

Your required sections depend on the CDL class, vehicle, endorsements, and restrictions involved.

  • Introduction and licensing rules: CDL classes, endorsements, restrictions, disqualifications, and application requirements.
  • Driving Safely: inspections, vehicle control, speed, space management, hazards, railroad crossings, emergencies, and difficult conditions.
  • Transporting Cargo Safely: weight, balance, securement, inspections, and cargo-related risks.
  • Passenger Vehicles: loading, unloading, prohibited practices, emergency equipment, and passenger safety.
  • Air Brakes: air-system components, pressure, warning devices, spring brakes, leakage tests, and safe braking techniques.
  • Combination Vehicles: coupling systems, trailer air lines, fifth wheels, off-tracking, rollover prevention, and inspections.
  • Doubles and Triples: converter dollies, coupling order, handling, and inspection procedures.
  • Tank Vehicles: liquid surge, outage, vehicle stability, and safe operation.
  • Hazardous Materials: placards, shipping papers, loading, parking, security, and emergency procedures.
  • School Buses: student loading zones, warning systems, railroad crossings, inspections, and emergency procedures.
  • Skills testing: vehicle inspection, basic control skills, and on-road driving.

Florida CDL classes

Florida issues Class A, Class B, and Class C commercial driver licenses. The required class depends on the vehicle’s weight ratings, trailer weight, passenger capacity, and hazardous-material use.

  • Class A: Generally applies to qualifying combinations with a gross combination weight rating of 26,001 pounds or more when the towed unit exceeds 10,000 pounds.
  • Class B: Generally applies to qualifying single vehicles with a gross vehicle weight rating of 26,001 pounds or more and certain vehicles towing 10,000 pounds or less.
  • Class C: Applies to certain vehicles below Class A or B weight thresholds when they transport placardable hazardous materials or are designed to transport 16 or more people, including the driver.

Florida age requirements

Florida CDL applicants must generally be at least 18 years old. Drivers younger than 21 are restricted to commercial operation within Florida.

Intrastate-only restriction A Florida CDL holder younger than 21 generally cannot use the CDL for interstate commercial driving. Interstate and hazardous-material operations are subject to additional federal age requirements.

Florida commercial learner’s permit

A commercial learner’s permit allows the applicant to practice operating the appropriate class of commercial vehicle while accompanied by a properly licensed CDL holder.

Applicants generally must hold a valid Florida operator license, satisfy vision and documentation requirements, and pass the applicable CDL knowledge tests.

The CLP must be held for at least 14 days before the applicant may take the applicable CDL skills test.

The application process may include:

  1. Confirm that you meet Florida residency requirements.
  2. Hold a valid Florida driver license.
  3. Provide required identity and Social Security documentation.
  4. Provide required Florida residential-address documents.
  5. Provide medical-certification information when required.
  6. Pass the vision requirement.
  7. Pass the required CDL knowledge examinations.
  8. Pay the applicable license and endorsement fees.

Florida residency documents for CDL applicants

Florida requires CDL and CLP applicants to provide documentation that is more specific than the standard address-certification process used for some noncommercial credentials.

Applicants should review the current FLHSMV list carefully before visiting an office. Florida states that CLP and CDL applicants cannot use the standard Certification of Address form as a substitute for the required CDL residential-address documentation.

Florida CDL knowledge tests

The exact tests depend on the requested CDL class, vehicle, endorsements, and restrictions.

Possible examinations include:

  • General Knowledge.
  • Air Brakes.
  • Combination Vehicles.
  • Passenger Transport.
  • School Bus.
  • Tank Vehicles.
  • Doubles and Triples.
  • Hazardous Materials.

Florida states that CDL knowledge tests may be available in multiple languages and may be administered orally in English or Spanish, except for the hazardous-materials test. Skills testing must be completed in English, and interpreters are not permitted during the test.

Do not study only practice questions Practice questions help reveal weak areas, but the Florida CDL handbook is the main source for the rules, procedures, and safety concepts tested.

Entry-Level Driver Training

Federal Entry-Level Driver Training requirements apply to many applicants seeking a first Class A or Class B CDL, upgrading from Class B to Class A, or obtaining a first-time HazMat, passenger, or school bus endorsement.

When ELDT applies, training must be completed through a provider listed in the FMCSA Training Provider Registry.

Florida verifies applicable ELDT completion before the CDL skills test. For a first-time HazMat endorsement, training must be verified before the HazMat knowledge test.

Florida medical certification

CDL applicants must declare the type of commercial operation they perform or expect to perform. Depending on that self-certification category, the driver may be required to maintain a valid Medical Examiner’s Certificate.

Interstate non-excepted commercial drivers generally must be examined by a medical examiner listed on the National Registry of Certified Medical Examiners.

Medical certification must remain current when required. An expired medical status may affect the driver’s commercial eligibility.

Florida CDL skills test

The CDL skills test evaluates whether the applicant can inspect, control, and safely operate a representative commercial vehicle.

The test generally includes:

  • Vehicle inspection: identifying safety-critical components and explaining what is being inspected.
  • Basic control skills: completing required low-speed maneuvers within the testing area.
  • Road test: demonstrating safe commercial driving in traffic and roadway situations.

Applicants must test in an appropriate representative vehicle. Testing in a vehicle without required equipment or vehicle characteristics may result in restrictions being placed on the CDL.

Florida third-party CDL testing

Florida authorizes contracted third-party administrators and testers to administer commercial driver license skills tests under FLHSMV oversight.

Testing locations, services, fees, available vehicle classes, and whether a testing vehicle is provided vary by site. Confirm those details directly with the provider before scheduling.

Florida HazMat endorsement

A hazardous materials endorsement requires more than passing the state knowledge examination.

The process may include:

  • First-time HazMat Entry-Level Driver Training.
  • Passing the Florida HazMat knowledge test.
  • Providing required lawful-status and identity documentation.
  • Completing TSA fingerprinting and a security threat assessment.
  • Maintaining the required medical and CDL qualifications.

Review Florida HazMat endorsement requirements

Passenger and school bus endorsements

Passenger and school bus applicants may need additional knowledge tests, permit restrictions, Entry-Level Driver Training, skills testing, background checks, medical qualifications, and employer or school-district requirements.

Applicants should study both the passenger-vehicle and school-bus handbook sections when seeking an S endorsement because the school bus path commonly involves both passenger and school-bus qualifications.

Common Florida CDL restrictions

The vehicle used for the skills test can affect the restrictions shown on the CDL.

  • K restriction: Limits the driver to intrastate commercial operation.
  • L restriction: Prohibits operation of a commercial vehicle equipped with air brakes.
  • Z restriction: Restricts operation of commercial vehicles equipped with full air brakes.
  • E restriction: Restricts the driver from operating a manual-transmission commercial vehicle.
  • O restriction: Restricts the driver from operating a Class A tractor-trailer configuration.
  • M or N restrictions: Limit the class of passenger vehicle the CDL holder may operate.

Use a representative vehicle that matches the commercial operation you expect to perform whenever possible.

How to study the Florida handbook

  1. Choose the correct CDL class. Determine whether you need Class A, B, or C.
  2. Identify every endorsement. List HazMat, Tank, Passenger, School Bus, or Doubles and Triples.
  3. Study General Knowledge first. Build your foundation in inspections, hazards, speed, space, and emergencies.
  4. Add Air Brakes when applicable. Learn the system components and inspection procedure, not just pressure numbers.
  5. Add Combination Vehicles for Class A. Focus on coupling, trailer air systems, rollover risk, and inspections.
  6. Study each endorsement separately. Keep HazMat, Tank, Passenger, School Bus, and Doubles and Triples in separate review sessions.
  7. Practice by topic. Use missed questions to identify the handbook section that needs review.
  8. Prepare separately for the skills test. Practice inspection language, backing exercises, and road-test habits in a representative vehicle.

Florida CDL study checklist

  • Read the current Florida CDL handbook.
  • Confirm your CDL class and endorsements.
  • Verify Florida residency documentation.
  • Check whether ELDT applies.
  • Complete required medical certification.
  • Pass the applicable knowledge tests.
  • Hold the CLP for the required period.
  • Practice inspection procedures aloud.
  • Practice basic control maneuvers.
  • Schedule an approved Florida CDL skills test.
